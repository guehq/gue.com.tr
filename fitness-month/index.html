What is MET-based Scoring?

MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) is a unit that estimates how much energy the body uses during different physical activities. Each activity has a baseline MET value, and when available, the pace of the activity is used to assign a more accurate MET rating. The MET score is calculated by multiplying the activity's duration by its MET value. This scoring system allows us to evaluate workouts not just by time, but by effort intensity as well.

What Do MET Scores Represent?

  • Higher MET value ‚Üí more energy burned per minute.
  • Longer duration ‚Üí higher total score.
  • This means short, high-effort workouts can score similarly to long, moderate ones.
